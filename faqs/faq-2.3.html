Checking Employee Leave Balance: [Updated as of May-2024]

Easily Access Information Regarding Your Remaining Leave Entitlements to Plan Your Time Off Appropriately.


Monitoring your leave balance is crucial for planning your time off effectively. Here's how you can easily access information regarding your remaining leave entitlements:



1. Understanding Leaves and Leave Balance:
• Your leave balance consists of various types of leaves, including Casual Leave (CL), Sick Leave (SL), and Compensatory Off (COMP.OFF).
• Each month, 1 SL and 1 CL are allotted to your leave balance, ensuring you have access to essential time off throughout the year (12 SL and 12 CL).
• It's important to keep track of your leave balance to ensure you have sufficient leave entitlements for planned absences.



2. Accessing Leave Balance Information:
• Navigate to the dashboard of your HRMS platform, where your leave balance information is prominently displayed.
• You can also access your leave balance details through My Leaves page within the HRMS platform.
• Review your total leave balance, which includes the cumulative total of CL, SL, and COMP.OFF leaves available to you.

3. Understanding Leave Balance Components:
• Your leave balance typically consists of separate components for CL, SL, and COMP.OFF leaves.
• Casual Leave (CL) and Sick Leave (SL) are combined to form your total leave entitlement for regular time off.
• Compensatory Off (COMP.OFF) leave balance is separate and is accrued based on overtime work or additional hours logged.

4. Planning Your Time Off:
• Regularly check your leave balance to ensure you have sufficient entitlements for planned time off.
• Use your leave balance information to schedule time off for personal or professional reasons, ensuring proper coverage and workflow management.
• Communicate with your manager or HR department regarding your planned time off and submit leave requests in advance for approval.

By following these steps and actively monitoring your leave balance, you can plan your time off appropriately and ensure a healthy work-life balance.
