Terminating or Managing Resignations of Employees: [Updated as of May-2024]

Navigate Employee Departures and Offboarding Processes With Efficiency and Sensitivity, Minimizing Disruption and Ensuring Legal Compliance.


Terminating or managing resignations of employees involves navigating employee departures and offboarding processes with efficiency and sensitivity, while ensuring minimal disruption and legal compliance.
Here's a detailed guide on how to manage employee departures:


1.Terminating or Managing Resignations of Employees:
• Navigate to the Employee Management Tab: From the sidebar menu on the dashboard, access the employee management tab.
• Employee Overview: In the employee management section, locate the table displaying employee details. Click on the "View More" button associated with the specific employee who is resigning or being terminated.
• Accessing Employee Active Status: On the employee overview page, the employee's active status is prominently displayed at the top right corner.
• Changing Employee Status: Click on the active status indicator to initiate the process of changing the employee's status.
• Modal for Status Update: Upon clicking the active status indicator, a modal window will appear, providing options to update the employee's status.
• Selecting Termination or Resignation: Choose the appropriate status option based on whether the employee is resigning voluntarily or being terminated.
• Providing Details: Enter additional details, such as the reason for termination or resignation, effective date, and any relevant notes or comments.
• Confirming Changes: Review the details carefully and confirm the status update to proceed with the termination or resignation process.
• Offboarding Process: Upon confirmation, initiate the offboarding process, which may include collecting company assets, disabling access to systems and facilities, conducting exit interviews, and facilitating the transfer of responsibilities.
• Legal Compliance: Ensure that the termination or resignation process complies with applicable labor laws, regulations, and company policies to mitigate legal risks.
• Sensitivity and Support: Handle the offboarding process with sensitivity and provide support to the departing employee during their transition, maintaining a positive employer-employee relationship.


By following these steps, organizations can effectively manage employee departures, minimize disruption, and ensure legal compliance while demonstrating sensitivity and support for departing employees.
