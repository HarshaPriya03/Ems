Dealing with Incorrect Biometric Punches: [Updated as of May-2024]

Provide Guidance on Corrective Actions to Take When an Employee Incorrectly Punches Their Biometric Data to Ensure Accurate Attendance Records.


Dealing with Incorrect Biometric Punches involves providing guidance on corrective actions to take when an employee incorrectly punches their biometric data to ensure accurate attendance records.



1. Access the Attendance Log: Navigate to the dashboard sidebar where the Attendance option is located. Click on it to access the attendance table. Look for the attendance log option in the navigation bar.



2. Identify Incorrect Punches: In the attendance log, review the records to identify any instances of incorrect biometric punches. Look for discrepancies such as duplicate entries, missing punches, or incorrect timestamps.



3. Contact the Employee: Reach out to the employee who made the incorrect punch. Call or text them to notify them of the error and inquire about the reason behind the mistake.



4. Investigate if Necessary: If the reason for the incorrect punch is unclear or if it seems to be a recurring issue, investigate further. Determine if there are any underlying issues causing the problem, such as technical issues with the biometric system or misunderstandings about attendance procedures.


5. Seek IT Department Help: If needed, seek assistance from the IT department to resolve any technical issues with the biometric system or to troubleshoot any problems related to attendance recording.


6. Impose Penalties if Necessary: If the incorrect punches persist despite guidance and assistance, consider imposing penalties as per company policies. Inform the employee that repeated instances of incorrect punches may result in deductions from their salary, such as a penalty or Loss of Pay (LOP). Clearly communicate the consequences to the employee to ensure compliance with attendance procedures in the future.


By following these steps, you can effectively address and rectify incorrect biometric punches to maintain accurate attendance records and ensure adherence to company policies.
