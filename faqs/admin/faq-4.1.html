Manually Checking-In an Employee: [Updated as of May-2024]

Record Attendance Exceptions and Ensure Accurate Timekeeping Records in Cases Where Automated Systems Are Unavailable or Inaccurate.


Manually Checking-In an Employee: This process is essential in cases where automated systems are unavailable or inaccurate, such as when an employee forgets to check-in, there's an incorrect punch, or there's a fault in the CAMS biometric instrument.



1. Navigate to Attendance Management: Access the dashboard sidebar where the Attendance option is located. Click on it to proceed to the Attendance Management section.



2. Record Attendance: In the Attendance Management section, you'll find a table displaying attendance records. Look for the Check-In option in the navigation bar. Click on it to initiate the manual check-in process.



3. Enter Details: Once you've selected the Check-In option, a form will appear where you can enter the employee's name, date, time, and reason for the manual check-in. Ensure all details are accurate and relevant.



4. Submit: After filling out the necessary information, click on the Submit button to record the manual check-in. The system will then update the attendance records accordingly.


5. Verification: Verify that the manual check-in has been successfully recorded by checking the attendance table. The employee's check-in should now be visible, along with the details provided during the manual entry process.


By following these steps, you can effectively record attendance exceptions and ensure accurate timekeeping records even when automated systems are unavailable or unreliable.
